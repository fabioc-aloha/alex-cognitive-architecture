# The Research Paper I Couldn't Write Alone

*A graduate student's experience using Alex for academic writing*

---

## The Problem

I had three weeks to write a literature review for my dissertation proposal. The topic — AI-assisted decision making in healthcare — had hundreds of relevant papers. I'd read maybe forty of them. I had notes everywhere. I had no structure.

I'd stare at the blank document and feel paralyzed.

## How Alex Helped

### Finding the Structure

I started by describing my problem to Alex:

> "I need to write a lit review on AI decision support in healthcare. I have notes on 40 papers but no organizing framework. Help me think through this."

Alex didn't write my outline for me. Instead, it asked questions:

- What are the main debates in this field?
- Which papers disagree with each other?
- What gaps have you noticed in the research?

Through that conversation, I realized my papers fell into three camps: those focused on clinical accuracy, those focused on physician trust, and those focused on patient outcomes. That became my structure.

### The Citation Workflow

Alex has a skill for academic writing that understands citation formats. When I wrote:

> "Smith et al. found that physicians override AI recommendations 34% of the time"

Alex would ask:

> "Is that (Smith et al., 2023) or a different Smith? I see you cited a Smith 2023 earlier on trust factors."

It caught duplicate citations, inconsistent formatting, and claims I'd made without sources. Not by magic — by actually reading what I wrote and checking it against what I'd said before.

### The Writing Partner

The hardest part of academic writing is the first draft. Alex helped by being a sounding board:

- I'd write a messy paragraph explaining an idea
- Alex would summarize what I was trying to say
- I'd confirm or correct
- Alex would suggest clearer phrasing

It never wrote *for* me. But it helped me write faster because I wasn't alone with my thoughts.

## What I Learned

1. **Start with conversation, not writing.** Talking through your ideas helps you find structure.

2. **Let Alex catch the mechanical stuff.** Citation formatting, consistency checks, APA style — these are exactly the things AI is good at.

3. **Keep your voice.** Alex adapts to your writing style. It won't make you sound generic unless you let it.

## The Result

I finished the literature review in two weeks. My advisor said it was the most organized first draft she'd seen from me. The revision process was smoother because the structure was solid from the start.
