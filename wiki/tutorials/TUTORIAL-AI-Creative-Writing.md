# Tutorial: AI-Assisted Creative Writing
![AI creative writing](images/tutorial-ai-creative-writing.png)
*30 minutes · For Writers*

---

## What You'll Build

A creative writing workflow using AI for plot development, character building, dialogue, and revision — without losing your unique voice.

After this tutorial, you'll have a collaborator for the parts of writing where you get stuck.

---

## 📋 Prerequisites

- Alex installed in VS Code
- A writing project (story, novel, screenplay)
- Understanding of your genre and audience

---

## Why AI-Assisted Creative Writing?

AI helps with:
- Breaking through writer's block
- Generating alternatives (names, plot options, dialogue variations)
- Maintaining consistency across long works
- Revision and feedback
- Research for authentic details

AI doesn't replace:
- Your creative vision
- Your unique voice
- The emotional truth of your story

## 📍 Steps

### Step 1: Set Your Creative Context

```
I'm writing [genre] fiction.
Target audience: [who reads this]
Tone: [humorous, dark, literary, commercial, etc.]
Influences/comp titles: [similar works]
Current project: [brief description]
```

✅ **Checkpoint**: Alex understands your creative space.

---

### Step 2: Plot Development

When stuck on plot:

```
My protagonist [description] needs to [goal].
The obstacle is [challenge].
What are 5 different ways this could resolve?
Include at least one option that surprises me.
```

For structure:

```
I'm writing a [length] story with this premise: [premise]
Draft a three-act structure with major plot points.
```

Challenge your plot:

```
What are the weaknesses in this plot?
Where might readers lose interest or disbelief?
```

---

### Step 3: Character Building

```
My character is [brief description].
Help me deepen them:
- What's their internal contradiction?
- What do they want vs. what do they need?
- What's their characteristic speech pattern?
- What's a detail that reveals character without stating it?
```

For dialogue voice:

```
[Character name] is [description].
Write 5 things they would say when [situation].
Make each one reveal something about their personality.
```

---

### Step 4: Scene Writing

When blocked on a scene:

```
I need to write a scene where [what happens].
The purpose is [what readers should feel or learn].
What's an unexpected way to open this scene?
```

For setting details:

```
My scene is set in [location, time period].
What are sensory details I could include?
Focus on: what would surprise readers about this place.
```

---

### Step 5: Revision

```
Here's a passage I've written:
[paste your writing]

Feedback I want:
- Does the pacing work?
- Is the dialogue natural?
- What's unclear?
- What could be cut?
```

Specific revision:

```
This passage feels flat. The problem might be [your guess].
Give me 3 ways to add energy without changing the content.
```

## 💡 Tips

### Name Generation

```
Generate 10 names for a [description] character.
Setting: [time/place]
Should feel: [quality — strong, mysterious, ordinary, etc.]
Avoid: [any patterns to avoid]
```

### Dialogue Alternatives

```
My character needs to say they're angry.
Give me 5 ways to show this without the word "angry" 
or any variation. Each should fit a different situation.
```

### Research for Authenticity

```
My character is a [profession] in [setting].
What details would make this authentic?
What mistakes do writers commonly make about this?
```

### Series Bible Maintenance

```
Here's what I've established about my world/characters:
[paste notes]

I'm about to write [scene].
Flag any potential continuity issues.
```

---

## ⚠️ Protecting Your Voice

### Use AI for Options, Not Decisions

Generate alternatives, but YOU choose which fits your story.

### Start with Your Draft

Write your version first, then use AI to improve it — not the reverse.

### Iterate Toward You

When AI output sounds generic:
```
This sounds like AI writing. 
Make it more [specific quality from your voice].
```

### Know When to Close AI

Some moments need pure human creativity. Trust that instinct.

## Creative Writing Templates

### Story Opening

```
My story opens with [character] in [situation].
Give me 3 opening lines that:
1. Start with action
2. Start with voice/attitude
3. Start with sensory detail
```

### Plot Problem Solving

```
I've written myself into a corner:
[describe the problem]
What are 3 ways to get out of this 
that I haven't considered?
```

### Character Interview

```
Interview my character [name] about [topic].
Ask them 5 questions.
Write their answers in their voice.
```

## Genre-Specific Tips

### Mystery
```
I need to plant a clue that [reveals X].
How can I hide it in plain sight?
```

### Romance
```
My leads need romantic tension in this scene.
They can't [obvious trope].
What's an unexpected source of chemistry?
```

### Fantasy/Sci-Fi
```
This world has [unique element].
What are second-order consequences I might not have considered?
```
