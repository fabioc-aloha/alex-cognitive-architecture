# Tutorial: AI-Assisted Job Search

*30 minutes · For Job Seekers*

---

## What You'll Build

A complete job search workflow — resume tailoring, cover letters, LinkedIn optimization, portfolio presentation, and interview preparation.

After this tutorial, you'll present yourself more effectively for the roles you want.

---

## 📋 Prerequisites

- Alex installed in VS Code
- Your current resume or work history
- Target roles or companies

---

## Why AI-Assisted Job Search?

Job searching is repetitive knowledge work — exactly where AI helps most:

- Tailoring resumes to specific job descriptions
- Writing cover letters that don't sound generic
- Preparing for behavioral interviews
- Researching companies and interviewers
- Following up professionally

## 📍 Steps

### Step 1: Define Your Target

```
I'm a [current role] with [X years] experience in [field].
I'm targeting [role type] at [company type/size/industry].
My strengths: [list 3-5]
I want to move from [current situation] to [goal].
```

Ask Alex to clarify your positioning:

```
Based on my background, what roles am I best positioned for?
What gaps might I need to address?
```

✅ **Checkpoint**: You have a clear target and understand your positioning.

---

### Step 2: Resume Tailoring

Paste a job description:

```
Here's a job description I'm applying to:
[paste JD]

Here's my current resume:
[paste resume]

How should I tailor my resume for this role?
What keywords am I missing?
What experience should I emphasize?
```

Generate bullet points:

```
Rewrite my experience bullet for [company/role] to:
- Start with action verb
- Include quantified results
- Match the language of the job description
```

---

### Step 3: Cover Letter

```
Write a cover letter for [company] [role].
Opening: Why I'm excited about this specific company.
Middle: 2-3 relevant experiences that match key requirements.
Close: Specific ask and next steps.
Tone: Professional but personable, not generic.
```

Iterate:

```
The opening is too generic. 
What's unique about [company] I could reference?
```

---

### Step 4: LinkedIn Optimization

```
Review my LinkedIn headline and summary:
[paste current]

For someone targeting [role type], what should I change?
```

Generate improved versions:

```
Write 3 headline options that:
- State what I do and for whom
- Include relevant keywords
- Differentiate me from others with the same title
```

---

### Step 5: Interview Preparation

**Behavioral Questions:**

```
I have an interview for [role] at [company].
Generate 10 likely behavioral questions based on:
- The job description: [paste]
- The company values: [research or paste]
```

**STAR Stories:**

```
Help me structure a STAR response for:
Question: "Tell me about a time you [situation]"
My experience: [brief description]
```

**Company Research:**

```
What should I know about [company] before my interview?
Recent news, challenges, competitors, culture signals.
```

## 💡 Tips

### Salary Negotiation

```
I received an offer for [amount] for [role] at [company].
Market rate for this role in [location] is [range].
Help me draft a negotiation response.
```

### Following Up

```
Write a thank-you email after my interview with [person/role].
Reference: [specific topic we discussed]
Tone: Gracious, not desperate.
```

### Rejection Response

```
I was rejected from [company]. 
Write a professional response that:
- Thanks them for consideration
- Asks for feedback
- Leaves door open for future opportunities
```

### Portfolio Presentation

```
I'm presenting my portfolio for a [role] interview.
Projects: [list]
Help me structure a 15-minute walkthrough that:
- Shows range
- Demonstrates thinking process
- Ends with strongest work
```

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Generic Applications

Mass-applying with the same resume fails. Tailor for each role:
- Match keywords
- Emphasize relevant experience
- Show you understand the company

### Over-Reliance on AI

Interviewers can detect AI-written responses. Use AI for:
- Structure and drafting
- Idea generation

Then put it in YOUR voice.

### Ignoring ATS

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems:

```
Is my resume ATS-friendly?
Check for: formatting issues, keyword gaps, header problems.
```
