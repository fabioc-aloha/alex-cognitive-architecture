# Tutorial: AI-Assisted Academic Research

*35 minutes · For Researchers*

---

## What You'll Build

A research workflow using AI for literature discovery, synthesis, gap identification, and writing — while maintaining academic integrity.

After this tutorial, you'll accelerate research without compromising rigor.

---

## 📋 Prerequisites

- Alex installed in VS Code
- A research question or topic
- Understanding of your field's norms

---

## Why AI-Assisted Research?

AI helps with:
- Literature synthesis across hundreds of papers
- Identifying gaps in existing research
- Structuring arguments
- Drafting and revising prose
- Catching logical inconsistencies

AI does NOT replace:
- Original thinking
- Primary data collection
- Ethical judgment
- Domain expertise

---

## 📍 Steps

### Step 1: Frame Your Research Question

Start with context:

```
I'm researching [topic] for my [dissertation/paper/grant].
My field is [discipline]. My advisor specializes in [area].
Research question: [your RQ]
What I know so far: [brief summary]
```

Alex can help refine the question:

```
Is this research question specific enough? 
How would you narrow it for a 6-month study?
```

✅ **Checkpoint**: You have a well-scoped, answerable research question.

---

### Step 2: Literature Mapping

```
What are the major theoretical frameworks for [topic]?
Who are the key authors I should be reading?
What are the landmark papers in this area?
```

Build a structured literature map:

```
Create a table of the 10 most influential papers on [topic]:
| Paper | Authors | Year | Key contribution | How it relates to my RQ |
```

---

### Step 3: Identify Gaps

```
Based on this literature, what gaps exist?
Where is the research thin?
What questions remain unanswered?
```

Position your work:

```
How does my research question address a gap 
that existing literature doesn't cover?
```

---

### Step 4: Structure Your Argument

```
Draft an outline for my literature review:
- Major themes to cover
- Logical flow from broad to specific
- Where my contribution fits
```

Refine iteratively:

```
The transition from section 2 to 3 feels weak.
How can I connect [theme A] to [theme B] more clearly?
```

---

### Step 5: Draft and Revise

Work section by section:

```
Draft the methods section. 
My study design: [describe]
Sample: [describe]
Data collection: [describe]
Analysis approach: [describe]
```

Then revise:

```
Review this draft for:
- Clarity of explanation
- Logical gaps
- Missing justifications
- APA style compliance
```

## 💡 Tips

### Synthesizing Multiple Sources

```
I have notes on these 5 papers about [topic]:
[paste summaries]

Synthesize the key themes. Where do they agree? Disagree?
```

### Grant Writing

```
I'm applying for [grant name] with a $[amount] budget.
The funder prioritizes [values].
Help me frame my research to align with their priorities.
```

### Peer Review Response

```
I received this reviewer feedback: [paste]
Help me draft a response that:
- Addresses each point
- Explains my revisions
- Politely pushes back where appropriate
```

### Citation Management

```
Format these references in APA 7th edition:
[paste raw citation info]
```

## ⚠️ Academic Integrity

### Disclosure

Many journals now require AI use disclosure. Check your target venue's policy.

### Original Contribution

AI can help articulate your ideas, but the ideas must be yours. If AI generated the hypothesis, it's not your research.

### Verification

AI can hallucinate citations. Always verify:
- Paper exists
- Authors are correct
- Claims match the source

### Attribution

Quote directly when using AI-generated text. Paraphrase and integrate for your own prose.
